Tribute to Steven Paul Jobs



Steve Jobs was a visionary entrepreneur and co-founder of Apple Inc. He was known for his creativity, innovation, and impact on the technology industry.

About The Legend

Steve Jobs was born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California, to Joanne Schieble and Abdulfattah Jandali.
He was adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs.
In 1976, Jobs co-founded Apple Computer, Inc. with Steve Wozniak in his parents' garage.
Their first product was the Apple I computer.
Apple's breakthrough came with the Apple II, a personal computer that was a major success in the late 1970s and early 1980s.
Jobs left Apple in 1985 after a power struggle. He returned in 1997 when Apple acquired his company, NeXT Computer.
Under Jobs' leadership, Apple introduced iconic products like the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad, revolutionizing consumer technology.
Jobs emphasized sleek design and simplicity, which became hallmarks of Apple products.
Jobs was also involved in the animation industry, serving as the CEO of Pixar Animation Studios and becoming Disney's largest shareholder.
Jobs battled health issues, including pancreatic cancer, which led to his resignation as Apple's CEO in 2011.
Steve Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011, leaving behind a legacy of innovation and entrepreneurship that continues to influence the tech industry.
